Technology

The market of products based on the Artificial Intelligence family of algorithms is dynamic and very fast growing. The spectrum of applications of the new technology is vast, and any new breakthrough in the technology immediately leads to new products and even whole new market areas.

Big companies, such as Google, Microsoft, Amazon, IBM, etc. are investing a lot of time and resources into the development of AI technologies in order to stay on top and get the biggest part of the new markets. The fastest growing areas of AI applications include: search, translation, recommender systems, expert systems, algorithmic trading, robotics and autonomous systems, self-driving cars, fraud detection, terrorism prediction, etc. The technologies and related markets are booming despite the overall economic slowdown.

However what may look like a revolution, is not always based on the revolutionary technologies. Significant success of the Intelligent (Machine Learning) algorithms in the recent years is to the large extent due to the rapidly growing computational power of the modern computers. Programs can handle even bigger amounts of data and process them faster. But faster computation doesn’t necessarily mean higher degree of intelligence. There are some principal limitations of the modern ML algorithms, which will make a qualitative leap hardly possible in the nearest time.

Everybody who uses from time to time google translation tool, could not help been impressed how significantly has the quality of translation improved in the recent years. Nearly every day translation becomes smarter, the translated text gets closer to the ideal. One may be tempted to extrapolate this trend and to conclude that in a couple of years we won’t be able to tell the difference between human and automatic translation. Yet a deeper thought could question this optimistic view. A translation system uses a statistical analysis in order to learn most probable combinations of words based on their occurrences in texts, as well as most common correspondences between those combinations in different languages based on the statistics available from different examples of translation. There must be hundreds of thousands of words in a modern language and many more firm combinations, phrases and other predefined sequences of words in a sentence. This is what an artificial neural network learns to recognise and to use for translation. But we understand that a truly good translation can only be based on the understanding of meaning of words. This means, one should teach the system to billions of different objects, concepts, notions, phenomena describing our world, and a combinatorial number of possible relations between different objects. We humans, know the meaning of most of the objects we are using in our speech since we learn them from many different sources, and despite one may think otherwise, most of these sources are not written texts. It is hypothetically possible to teach an artificial neural network to all objects surrounding us, but the scale of this task is so enormously huge that it could not be done with modern technologies in the nearest future. This combinatorial divergence can not be compensated even by a fast growing performance of the computers.

Our approach is based on a number of algorithmic and technological ideas, which introduce a deep modification to the traditional Machine Learning algorithms, and provide a way to overcome some of the limitations due to combinatorial divergence. In a sense it can be called a new generation AI engine. We strongly believe that the new approach should lead to a qualitative leap in the level of intelligence of the AI algorithms, it will allow us to solve much more challenging tasks, putting us in a favourable position in the market. New types of products, new level of reliability and a broader spectrum of solutions will become possible with the new algorithm.

Advantages of the new algorithm.

  • Major limitations of the modern technologies
    • Requires huge amount of training data (low convergence rate), mostly the data has to be labelled (too much of human involvement).
    • Instability: sensitivity to the training data, new training data may affect existing functionality, new recognition classes require complete re-training.
    • Implicit nature of the knowledge. Impossible knowledge manipulation, eg. combining the knowledge from different intelligent agents.
    • Fast (combinatorial?) growth of the model complexity with the size of the classification problem.
    • Lack of generosity; non-universal character of algorithms; ad-hoc tailoring of every specific task.
  • Our approach - key advantages of the technology
    • Explicit knowledge representation.
    • Knowledge manipulation: merging of knowledge from different intelligent agents.
    • Model complexity grows linearly with the size of the knowledge; allows virtually unlimited growth.
    • Logical inference: synthesis of the new higher level knowledge from existing one.
  • Our approach - key practical advantages
    • Stability: new data can only improve performance, no re-training needed.
    • Universality: same algorithm used for all applications.
    • Shorter and simpler development stage (development of concrete applications) due to stability and universality.
    • Unlimited growth of the knowledge base - any model can be extended indefinitely without affecting performance or stability.
    • Higher “intelligence” power, more challenging tasks, eg.
      • translation by meaning and not by the dictionary;
      • recognition of objects in context (autonomous systems);
      • intelligent decision making, based on common sense, statistics and logical inference.
    • Flexibility - the ability to handle abstract concepts, introduce new classes of objects, concepts and relations dynamically (even “on-the-fly”).
